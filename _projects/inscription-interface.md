# Inscription Interface

<p>Ancient South Indian elites loved writing things down on stone - usually land/resource grants, commemorations of war victories, and other declarations that helped them prop up and legitimize their regimes and maybe even feel good about themselves. These inscriptions are spread out in both time and place, and if you're an amateur historian, your best resources (as far as I know) are large, unwieldly compendiums that list them out: <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epigraphia_Carnatica">Epigraphia Carnatica</a> and <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Indian_Inscriptions">South Indian Inscriptions</a>.</p>

<p>While the inscriptions are technically usable, I find it unintuitive to read them in the order that Lewis Rice decided to record them in (he also made the puzzling decision to separate the locations and the dates for each inscription). My goal is to programmatically create a better way to visualize and think about this data: an interface where you can better understand these inscriptions geographically, thematically, and historically.</p>

<b>Status:</b> In Progress - lots of code, lots of wishing that there were just better raw data to begin with.
